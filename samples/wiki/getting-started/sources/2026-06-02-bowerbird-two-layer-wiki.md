The wiki needs two layers, kept strictly apart.

- Sources layer: one faithful note per saved post — what the author actually said, attributed, no editorializing.
- Concepts layer: synthesized articles across sources; every claim links back to a source note.
- "Mixing the layers is how knowledge bases rot. The moment a 'concept' contains a claim nobody actually made, you can't trust any of it."
- A linter can enforce the split mechanically: every concept claim cites a source note, every link resolves, every source carries author, url, and date.
