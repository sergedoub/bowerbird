The wiki needs two layers. Sources: one faithful note per saved post — what the author actually said, attributed, no editorializing. Concepts: synthesized articles across sources where every single claim links back to a source note.

Mixing the layers is how knowledge bases rot. The moment a "concept" contains a claim nobody actually made, you can't trust any of it.

A linter can enforce this mechanically: every concept claim must cite a source note, every link must resolve, every source must carry author, url, and date.
